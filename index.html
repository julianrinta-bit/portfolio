How I Think

The willingness to kill is the part most creative directors skip.

In 2023, I shut down a fitness channel that was generating $40K/month. The retention curve had flatlined at week 6. No amount of creative iteration was going to fix a structural audience problem. I redirected the team, the budget, and the production pipeline to three channels that were still climbing.

Within 90 days, total revenue was higher than if I'd kept the dying channel alive.

That's how I think about creative: every decision has a number attached. Avatar → barriers → hook → script → brief → test → iterate → scale. Or kill.

I've run this system across 50+ channels, 15 languages, and teams of 100+ people spanning 4 continents—all remote, years before remote work had a hashtag. I built my own tracking systems before the dashboards existed. I was making decisions on CTR and retention when most teams were still arguing about what "engagement" meant.
