How I Think

The willingness to kill is the part most directors skip.

When I arrived at Bored Panda, the international operation was one Lithuanian editor translating videos with Google Translate. When I left, it was 50+ channels across 15 languages generating $400K/month—with a team I built, systems I designed, and a P&L I owned.

In between, I shut down a channel generating $40K/month because the retention curve flatlined at week 6. Within 90 days, total revenue was higher than if I'd kept it alive.

That's how I think. Every decision starts with the audience and ends with a number. Or a kill.

I built Facebook pages when a single post could move a million views in a week. By the time YouTube had analytics dashboards, I'd already been tracking channels with custom systems—making calls on CTR, retention, and revenue attribution when those metrics barely had names.

How I Work

AI-Powered Revenue Systems

In 2022, I reduced operational costs 70% with AI. Not as an experiment. As a restructuring. I replaced people with systems and kept one human checkpoint, then rebuilt how a video goes from idea to published file—a full year before ChatGPT launched publicly.

The strategy is mine.
The AI handles the volume.
The humans handle the judgment.
01

Knowledge Architecture

I built a structured library—audience profiles, hook patterns, frameworks—indexed so AI agents can pull from it in seconds.

02

AI-Assisted Ideation

I set the angle. The system generates hooks, variants, scripts. I filter. The editor builds the final piece.

03

Revenue Systems

Channel diagnostics, competitor monitoring, automated research pipelines. I build the tools that surface what's working and flag what's dying—so decisions happen before damage does.

04

Human-in-the-Loop

Editors, designers, and talent make the final pieces. I architect the concept and the testing system. What performs gets scaled. What doesn't gets cut.
